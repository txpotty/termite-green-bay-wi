Detailed Explanation of Our Termite Control Services

Our comprehensive termite control services are designed to address infestations at their source and provide long-term protection for your home. We utilize the latest techniques and environmentally conscious products to ensure effective and safe treatment.

Expert Termite Inspection

The first step in effective termite control is a thorough inspection to identify the presence, extent, and species of termites infesting your property. Our trained technicians conduct detailed inspections of your home's interior and exterior.

What Our Inspection Includes

  • Exterior Assessment: We examine foundations, wooden structures, patios, decks, and landscaping for signs of termite activity, such as mud tubes, damaged wood, and discarded wings.

  • Interior Assessment: Inside your home, we inspect basements, crawl spaces, attics, walls, and flooring for signs of infestation, including hollow-sounding wood, bubbling paint, and termite droppings (frass).

  • Moisture Detection: We assess areas with potential moisture issues, as moisture is a significant attractant for termites.

Based on the findings of the inspection, we develop a customized treatment plan tailored to your specific situation.

Targeted Termite Treatments

Once the inspection is complete, our team implements targeted treatments to eradicate the termite colony and protect your home from future infestations. Our methods are chosen based on the termite species, the severity of the infestation, and your property's specific characteristics.

Common Treatment Methods We Employ

  • Liquid Termiticide Barriers: We apply liquid termiticide to the soil around your home's foundation, creating a barrier that termites cannot penetrate. This treatment is highly effective and provides long-lasting protection.

  • Termite Baiting Systems: Baiting systems involve strategically placing bait stations around your property. Termites feed on the bait and carry it back to the colony, effectively eliminating the entire colony.

  • Wood Treatments: For localized infestations or to protect specific wooden structures, we may apply specialized wood treatments.

  • Fumigation (in specific cases): While less common for subterranean termites, fumigation may be recommended for drywood termite infestations in certain situations. This involves enclosing the structure and introducing a fumigant gas to eliminate pests.

Common Termite Problems and How We Solve Them

Recognizing the signs of a termite infestation early is crucial to minimizing damage. Many homeowners in De Pere and surrounding areas encounter specific issues that signal the presence of these destructive pests. Understanding these signs can prompt you to seek professional help before the problem escalates.

Mud Tubes on Foundation or Walls

Subterranean termites, the most common type in Wisconsin, build mud tubes as protective pathways to travel between their underground colonies and food sources (your home). These tubes are typically about the width of a pencil and can be found on the exterior or interior of your foundation or walls.

Finding mud tubes is a definite indicator of termite activity. Even if the tube appears empty, it may have been recently used or could be a sign of active exploration. Our professional inspection will confirm the presence of termites and determine the extent of the infestation based on the number and location of these tubes. Once confirmed, we utilize targeted treatments like liquid barriers or baiting systems to eradicate the colony responsible for building these structures, effectively halting their access to your home and preventing further tube construction.

Hollow-Sounding Wood

Termites consume wood from the inside out, leaving the outer shell intact. As they tunnel through wooden structures like walls, door frames, or window sills, the wood becomes hollow. Tapping on affected areas will produce a dull, hollow sound unlike solid wood.

This is a more advanced sign of infestation, indicating that termites have been active in the area for some time. Our inspectors are trained to identify subtle changes in the sound of wood. If we detect hollow-sounding wood, we investigate further to confirm termite presence using specialized tools like moisture meters and thermal cameras. Our treatment addresses the active termites within the damaged wood and implements preventive measures to protect surrounding areas from future attacks, often involving direct treatment of the affected wood and the creation of protective barriers.

Termite Swarms and Discarded Wings

Swarming termites, also known as alates, are reproductive termites that leave the colony in large numbers to establish new colonies. These swarms often occur in the spring, especially after rain. You might see these flying insects around windows or light fixtures.

Termite swarming activity, or the presence of discarded wings (as termites shed their wings shortly after swarming), is a clear indication of a nearby mature termite colony. Even if the swarm is brief, the presence of wings suggests a colony is established on or near your property. We respond quickly to reports of swarms or discarded wings, conducting a thorough inspection to locate the source of the infestation. Our treatment focuses on eliminating the established colony and protecting your home from future swarming activity from that or other colonies, often combining barrier treatments and baiting systems for comprehensive control.

Bubbling or Peeling Paint

Termites, especially subterranean termites, often introduce moisture into the wood they infest. This added moisture can cause paint or wallpaper to bubble, peel, or flake. This can sometimes be mistaken for water damage, but it's essential to consider termites as a potential cause.

If you notice unexplained bubbling or peeling paint, particularly in areas with wood structures, it's worth investigating the possibility of a termite infestation. Our experienced technicians can differentiate between termite damage and water damage through careful inspection. If termites are the culprit, we address the active infestation causing the issue and advise on repairs to the damaged areas, ensuring the underlying pest problem is resolved before cosmetic fixes are made. Our goal is to eliminate the source of the moisture and wood damage.

Small Piles of What Looks Like Sawdust (Frass)

Drywood termites, less common in Wisconsin than subterranean termites but still a possibility, expel their fecal pellets, known as frass, from tiny kick-out holes in the wood they are infesting. These pellets resemble small piles of sawdust or sand.

While frass is more indicative of drywood termites, it's important to have any suspicious piles of what looks like sawdust inspected. Our technicians are trained to identify the specific type of termite infestation based on the visual evidence left behind. If drywood termites are confirmed, our treatment methods are tailored to this species, often involving localized treatments or, in severe cases, fumigation of the infested area or structure to ensure complete eradication of the colony within the wood.

Damaged or Discolored Drywall/Plaster

Termites can damage drywall and plaster as they tunnel behind it, particularly if the wood framing behind these materials is infested. This can lead to sagging, cracking, or discoloration of the wall surface. The damage may resemble water damage but without the presence of actual water.

Unexplained damage to drywall or plaster can be a sign of termite activity within the wall structure. Our inspection process includes examining walls for subtle signs of damage and using listening devices to detect termite activity within the walls. If termites are found, we utilize appropriate treatments to eliminate the infestation behind the wall and address any underlying structural damage caused by their activity, ensuring the wall is free from pests before any repairs are made. This often involves drilling small holes for targeted application of termiticides.

Tight-Fitting Doors and Windows

As termites consume wood and introduce moisture, wooden door and window frames can warp, making them difficult to open and close. This can be a subtle sign of an infestation within the frame itself.

If you notice doors or windows that suddenly become difficult to operate, especially during warm or humid weather (when termites are often more active), it could be a sign of an underlying termite problem. Our inspection examines door and window frames for signs of damage and listens for any sounds of termite activity. If termites are confirmed, we treat the infested frames and surrounding areas to eliminate the colony and prevent further damage that could compromise the functionality of your doors and windows. Addressing the infestation will also help prevent further warping.

Peeling Paint That Looks Like Water Damage

As mentioned previously, the moisture introduced by termites can lead to paint peeling in a way that mimics water damage. However, in cases of termite damage, the cause is the pest activity, not necessarily an external water source.

Differentiating between water damage and termite damage is critical for effective treatment. Our experienced technicians are skilled at recognizing the subtle differences. We utilize moisture meters to pinpoint areas of elevated moisture and inspect for other signs of termite activity like mud tubes or frass. If termites are the cause, our treatment focuses on eradicating the infestation and implementing measures to prevent future moisture issues caused by termites, allowing for proper repair of the paint and underlying structure.

Visible Termite Workers or Soldiers

While much of termite activity occurs hidden within wood or underground, you may occasionally see termite workers or soldiers when investigating damaged wood. Workers are typically pale or white and wingless, while soldiers have larger, darker heads and strong jaws (mandibles).

Seeing live termites is a clear and undeniable sign of an active infestation. This indicates a significant presence and requires immediate professional attention. Our rapid response team will conduct a thorough inspection to determine the extent of the colony. Treatment will involve aggressive measures to eliminate the visible termites and the rest of the colony, often using a combination of baiting and barrier treatments for maximum effectiveness. Your safety and the preservation of your home are our top concerns when active termites are visible.

Sagging Floors or Ceilings

Severe termite infestations can compromise the structural integrity of wooden support beams or joists, leading to sagging floors or ceilings. This is a sign of extensive and long-term damage and requires urgent professional intervention.

Sagging floors or ceilings represent significant structural damage and indicate a severe termite infestation that has impacted key support elements of your home. Our immediate action involves a comprehensive assessment of the structural damage and a rapid, aggressive treatment plan to eradicate the termite colony. We work to eliminate the active infestation entirely and can provide recommendations for structural repairs needed to address the damage caused by the termites. Addressing the pest problem is the essential first step before structural remediation can begin effectively. We understand the urgency of such a situation.

Subtle Clicking or Headbanging Sounds

Worker termites make clicking sounds within wooden structures as they chew, and soldier termites will bang their heads against tunnel walls to signal danger to the colony. While often difficult to detect, in quiet environments or when listening closely to infested wood, you might hear these subtle sounds.

Detecting these sounds requires a quiet environment and often specialized listening equipment, which our technicians are equipped with. If you hear unexplained clicking or tapping sounds coming from your walls or floors, it could be a very early indicator of termite activity. Our inspection process includes using sensitive equipment to confirm the presence of termites based on these sounds. If confirmed, we implement targeted treatments to eliminate the colony before the infestation becomes more widespread and causes visible damage. Early detection through these subtle signs can save you significant costs and stress in the long run.

Foundation Damage or Cracks

While termites don't eat concrete or brick, they can exploit even tiny cracks in foundations to gain entry to your home. Moisture accumulating in foundation cracks can also attract termites to the area, providing them with easy access to the wooden structures above.

Inspecting your foundation for cracks is an important part of preventing termite infestations. Our technicians examine your foundation for any potential entry points that termites could utilize. If we find cracks, we address the source of any nearby termite colonies and can recommend methods to seal these entry points as part of a comprehensive prevention plan. While we focus on termite elimination, we understand the importance of securing your home's structure against future invasions. Ensuring your foundation is free of cracks is a key defensive measure against these persistent pests.

Wingless Insects Resembling Ants During Spring

During the spring swarming season, winged termites are often mistaken for flying ants. However, there are key differences in their appearance. Termites have straight antennae, a broad waist, and two pairs of equally sized wings, while ants have elbowed antennae, a narrow waist, and forewings that are longer than their hindwings.

Misidentifying swarming termites as ants can lead to ineffective treatment and allow the infestation to spread. It's crucial to be able to differentiate between the two or, even better, call a professional. Our technicians are experts at identifying termites versus ants and can quickly determine if you have a termite problem based on observing the insects. If they are termites, we initiate immediate treatment to eliminate the swarming colony and address the source of the infestation, preventing the establishment of new colonies on or near your property. Accurate identification is the first step towards effective pest control.

Termite Droppings (Frass) Near Wood

As mentioned earlier, frass is a key indicator of drywood termites. Finding small piles of these hexagonal pellets near wooden objects, furniture, or structural wood is a strong sign of their presence. The color of the frass can vary depending on the type of wood being consumed.

Discovering frass is a strong indicator of a drywood termite infestation, a type of termite that lives entirely within the wood they infest. Our inspection includes searching for these tell-tale piles of pellets around susceptible wooden items. If frass is found, we confirm the presence of drywood termites and implement targeted treatments, which may include injecting termiticides directly into the infested wood or, in more extensive cases, recommending fumigation to ensure complete eradication of the colony living within the wood. Prompt action upon finding frass is essential to prevent further damage to valuable wooden structures and furniture.

Wood that CrumbLes Easily or Feels Spongy

Termite-damaged wood loses its structural integrity and becomes weak. If you press on infested wood, it may crumble easily or feel soft and spongy to the touch as the interior has been eaten away.

Wood that is crumbling or feels spongy is a clear sign of significant termite damage. This indicates that the infestation has progressed to the point where the wood's structural strength is compromised. Our inspection involves probing wooden elements to assess their integrity. If we find soft or crumbling wood, we confirm the presence of termites and immediately begin treatment to eliminate the active colony. We also assess the extent of the damage and can advise on the necessary structural repairs needed to restore the integrity of the affected areas once the termites are gone. Addressing both the pest and the damage is crucial for the safety and longevity of your home.

Musty or Mildewy Odor

Termite activity can sometimes produce a musty or mildewy odor, particularly in areas with high humidity. This odor is caused by the moisture termites introduce and the byproducts of their activity within the wood.

An unexplained musty or mildewy odor, especially in areas where wood is present, could be a subtle indicator of a termite infestation, particularly in conjunction with other signs. Our inspectors are trained to detect the unique odors associated with termite activity. If we notice this odor during an inspection, we investigate further to confirm the presence of termites and identify the source of the infestation. Eliminating the termites will help reduce the associated odor and prevent further moisture issues. We address both the pests and the contributing factors to create a healthy and pest-free environment within your home.

Electrical Outlets or Switches With Soil Or Mud

Termites can sometimes travel through wall voids and may even build mud tubes around electrical outlets or switches as they seek pathways through your home. Finding soil or mud around these electrical fixtures is a strong sign of subterranean termite activity.

The presence of soil or mud around electrical outlets or switches is a serious indicator of subterranean termite activity and requires immediate professional attention. Termites can cause damage to electrical wiring, creating potential fire hazards. Our top priority in such cases is to safely assess the situation, confirm the termite infestation, and implement targeted treatments to eliminate the termites before they cause any further damage to your electrical system. We take extreme caution when inspecting and treating areas near electrical components, prioritizing your safety above all else.

Pinpoint Holes in Wood Surfaces

While less common for subterranean termites, drywood termites create small kick-out holes in the surface of infested wood to expel their fecal pellets (frass). These holes are typically the size of a pinhead.

Pinpoint holes in wooden surfaces, especially with small piles of frass nearby, are a clear sign of a drywood termite infestation. Our inspection includes a close examination of wooden furniture, structural beams, and other wooden elements for these tiny holes. If found, we confirm the presence of drywood termites and implement appropriate treatment methods, such as localized injections or fumigation, to eliminate the colony living within the wood. Early detection of these small holes is vital to prevent significant damage to valuable wooden items and structures within your home.

Damaged Wood that Appears Chewed or Gnawed

Termite damage to wood can appear differently than damage caused by other pests. Termite-damaged wood often has a distinctive pattern of tunnels and galleries running through it, and the surfaces may appear chewed or gnawed, particularly on the interior.

Examining damaged wood carefully can help determine if termites are the culprit. Our experienced technicians can differentiate between termite damage and damage caused by other pests or factors like rot. If the damage pattern is consistent with termite activity, we confirm the infestation and implement a comprehensive treatment plan to eradicate the colony causing the damage.
